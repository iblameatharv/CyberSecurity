About CyberGuard

What is Cyber Security?

Cyber security is the practice of protecting computers, servers, networks, and data from digital attacks. These cyberattacks are usually aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information, extorting money from users, or interrupting normal business processes.

Implementing effective cybersecurity measures is particularly challenging today because there are more devices than people, and attackers are becoming more innovative.

Why It Matters

In our increasingly digital world, cybersecurity has never been more important. From personal data to critical infrastructure, nearly everything relies on computer systems and the internet.

Good cybersecurity helps protect:

  • Personal information and identity
  • Financial data and transactions
  • Business operations and intellectual property
  • National security and critical infrastructure

Cyber Security Chapters

Explore these 10 chapters to understand the fundamentals of cybersecurity

1 Introduction to Cyber Security

▼

Cyber security is the practice of protecting computers, servers, networks, and data from digital attacks. Hackers try to steal or destroy important data, but cyber security keeps information safe.

Why it matters: Protects personal data, keeps schools and banks safe, helps create a safe digital India.

2 Types of Cyber Threats

▼

Cyber threats are attacks that can harm computers or steal data. Common threats include viruses, worms, phishing, spyware, and ransomware.

Tip: Never click on unknown links or attachments in emails or messages.

3 Cyber Ethics & Netiquette

▼

Cyber ethics means using technology responsibly. Netiquette (Internet + Etiquette) refers to good manners online.

Good Cyber Ethics: Be polite and respectful online, never post hurtful comments, ask permission before sharing photos, report cyberbullying.

4 Password Protection

▼

Your password is your digital key. If someone gets it, they can enter your online world.

Tips for Strong Passwords: Use a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols; avoid personal information; change regularly; use 2-factor authentication.

Password Strength Checker

5 Data Encryption

▼

Encryption is the process of changing readable data into code to protect it from hackers. Only someone with the decryption key can read it.

Encryption keeps your chats, banking, and school data secure.

6 Safe Internet Browsing

▼

When using the internet: Always check if the website starts with HTTPS, never download from unknown sources, avoid pop-up ads, log out after using public computers.

Remember: Think before you click!

7 Cyber Laws in India

▼

The government created laws to protect people online. The main one is The Information Technology (IT) Act, 2000.

Under this law: Hacking and identity theft are punishable crimes, online bullying and harassment are illegal, data theft can lead to fines or jail.

8 Social Media Safety

▼

Social media helps us connect, but it can also be dangerous if misused.

Safety Tips: Keep your account private, don't accept friend requests from strangers, never share OTPs or personal details, think before posting pictures.

9 Cyberbullying & Online Harassment

▼

Cyberbullying is when someone uses the internet to hurt, threaten, or embarrass others.

If it happens: Don't reply to bullies, take screenshots, report to a teacher, parent, or cyber police.

10 The Future of Cyber Security

▼

The future will depend on smart defenders — people like you!

Trends: AI will detect hackers faster, blockchain will secure transactions, ethical hackers will protect systems.

Cyber Security Careers: Ethical Hacker, Security Analyst, Forensic Expert, Data Protection Officer.

Security Practices

1
Strong Passwords

Use complex, unique passwords for each account and enable two-factor authentication.

2
Regular Updates

Keep all software, especially security software, updated to patch vulnerabilities.

3
Backup Data

Regularly backup important data to recover quickly in case of ransomware or data loss.
